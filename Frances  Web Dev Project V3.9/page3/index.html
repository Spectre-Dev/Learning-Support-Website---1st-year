The Learning Support Service is available to all students registered with the Learning & Disability Support service. It offers an understandng supportive environment that recognises and help students to overcome their difficulties. The service helps you to adapt your learning by identifying your individual learning differences and supports you as you acquire the skills to learn independently. The service offers:

  • Small group workshops
  • Individual learning support
  • Proof-reading service
  • Information on useful support materials/resources

The Learning Support service provided by Catherine Elliott is tailored for those registered with the Learning & Disability Support (LDS) Service. You can register with the LDS service if you have a learning difficulty, a disability, a mental health difficulty or a significant ongoing illness. If you would like to register with the LDS service please contact Catherine (details above) or Karen Mooney, the Disability Officer, to make an appointment. If you require Learning Support and you are not registered with the LDS please contact Michael Goldrick who provides Learning Support to all students.

The Learning Support Service offers a numbers of small group workshops in Semester 1 & Semester 2 covering a number of topics such as:


  • Academic writing
  • Reading & writing skills
  • Learning styles & strategies
  • Time management
  • Exam strategies

These workshops are informal and it is a great opportunity to ask any questions or share any concerns you may have. The Learning Support service is also in attendance at the Learning Club.
